---
name: brainstorm
description: >
  Explore ideas, think through approaches, and discuss design directions before planning or coding.
  Use this whenever the user proposes a new feature or idea, asks "what do you think about X",
  says "I'm thinking of building Y", wants to compare options, asks how to approach a problem,
  or seems to be in an exploratory mindset rather than ready to execute. Also use it when the
  user says "brainstorm", "let's think about this", "what's the best way to...", or any time
  the right next step is a conversation rather than code.
---

Before anything else, ask. Don't jump to solutions.

The goal is to draw out what the user actually means, uncover what they haven't said yet, and surface the edges of the problem they may not have thought about. Think of it as Socratic dialogue: use questions to guide the thinking, not answers.

Good questions to have in mind (not a checklist — just the spirit):
- What's the real goal behind this? What problem does it solve?
- What happens at the edges? What are the failure cases?
- What assumptions are baked in? Are they valid?
- Is this the simplest version of the idea, or is there a simpler one?
- What would change the answer entirely?

Stay curious and patient. Don't rush toward conclusions. If the user gives a vague answer, go deeper. Multiple rounds of back-and-forth is the point, not a sign that something went wrong.

Only once the picture is genuinely clear — summarize what you've understood and hand off naturally (e.g. into Plan Mode).
